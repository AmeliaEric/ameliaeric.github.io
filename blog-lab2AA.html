LAB 2: Android Adventures

  • By:Amelia Eric-Markovic
  • Date:September 26th, 2023

C: The Emulator Chronicles

I used BlueStacks as my emulator because it was free and looked to have the best user experience. Downloading it was easy and took around 7 minutes in total, and around less than a minute to open up the .apk file we were given to test. In total honesty the first .apk I got to pick to try out did not work but it said that file was not downloaded properly so I ended up with my second choice of .apk which still worked fairly well.

I have a video shown here with this blog post that shows a run through of me testing the .apk files but I am going to provide a written run through of what I thought about testing because I could not record my video with audio. For the .apk we were provided, I automatically realized that I had a black pen but once I chose a colour I could never switch back to the black pen, which for some users could be annoying. Their is also no erasor tool which is simply poor UX but overall it did work smoothly and I only found that one error with the black pen colour. Switching over to the .apk that I chose, geometry dash was a very fun game to play but the mouse clicks did not align with the jumping which made the game very hard to play accuratly. In practice mode it has the ability to save your place in a game at harder parts but for some reason also saved your clicks, so I got caught in a jump loop that kept killing my character. The graphics gliched at the bottom of the screen at times but it looked just like the game I used to love as a kid, a very fun game but would have been far more fun with a reliable jump machanic.
